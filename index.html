The Five Objectives

The Battle for the Lexicon

Each objective addresses a front in the war to mutate Catholic understanding—from language to hierarchy to doctrine itself.

I

The Source of the Lexicon

Establish the Divine Source of all Truth. God as Pure Actuality (Actus Purus), the Trinity as Divine Super-Logic, and the Analogy of Being that prevents us from making God in our image. Without this foundation, the rest of the Lexicon has no ground to stand on.

II

The War of Words

Establish what the words used in Catholicism actually mean versus what they have been mutated to mean in modernist, secular society. Examine how terms like freedom, charity, dialogue, and development of doctrine have been weaponized against the Faith.

III

The Sacred Hierarchy

Identify the true function, authority, and responsibilities of the clergy—from deacon to pope. Understand apostolic succession, the curia, and the machinery of universal governance as established by Christ.

IV

The Shield of Faith

Understand the heresies refuted by the Church from the death of Christ to today. Study the Fathers, Doctors, and Councils who identified and defeated Arianism, Gnosticism, Pelagianism, Modernism, and more.

V

The Great Rupture

Identify the errors that motivated the Protestant "Reformation." Examine why they contradict 1,500+ years of Teaching and Tradition, and which areas of Scripture they violate.

VI

The Synthesis of Error

Knit together the full picture: how Evil has methodically worked for over a thousand years to mutate language, corrupt administrators, and introduce Protestant ideas into the Catholic Church—none of it endorsed by Christ.

The Curriculum

Six Modules. One Unbroken Thread.

Module II

The Shield of Faith

Module V

The Experience

How the Course Works

Each week combines rigorous intellectual formation with communal Socratic discussion—designed for adults and young adults ready to engage.

◈

Primary Reading

Approximately 45 minutes of curated reading from the Church Fathers, Doctors, Councils, and Scholastic masters. Sources provided, context established.

◉

Video Lectures

15–20 minutes of guided lecture content that frames the reading, highlights key arguments, and prepares you for discussion.

◎

Socratic Discussion

1 hour of open, guided discussion. Apply the Audit methodology to real-world examples. No question is off-limits—only intellectual dishonesty is challenged.
